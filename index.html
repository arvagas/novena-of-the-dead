Novena: Prayer for the Dead

What is a Novena?

A novena, in Roman Catholicism, is a successive, nine-day period of prayer, typically performed for special occasions or requesting special favors from God.

Why pray for the dead?

In Roman Catholicism, it is a way to ask God to accept those who died into heaven. We pray because we want God to forgive the person from any wrongdoings he/she/they may have done while alive, and grant them entrance into paradise.
